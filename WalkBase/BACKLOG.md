- **Emulator caveat:** the emulator has no real step sensor ‚Äî anything needing real step‚ÜíBrick earning can't be fully exercised here; implement + verify the UI/logic and note what needs a physical device.

## Decisions & Blockers
<!-- Heads-up for the user (AFK). Append: #N ‚Äî <decision made / assumption / workaround / what was deferred and why>. Don't stop the loop for these. -->
#1 ‚Äî Import/Restore uses Android's FilePicker; I couldn't exercise the native picker dialog on the emulator via adb, so the restore *flow* is verified by unit tests (serializer round-trip + junk rejection) and the atomic DB-replace logic, not an on-device file pick. Worth a manual restore test on your physical device.
#10 ‚Äî The widget provider is registered and its data path is verified (snapshot prefs written + read), but I couldn't *place* the widget on the home screen via adb (no reliable scripted way to drag a widget from the launcher picker). Please add it manually on your device (long-press home ‚Üí Widgets ‚Üí Walk Track) to see it render; it shows town name, Bricks, and step-goal progress, taps to open the app, and refreshes every ~30 min plus on each background step reconcile.
#9 ‚Äî Couldn't get a clean before/after CPU number on the emulator (dumpsys cpuinfo is noisy; the "28%" baseline was a launch transient ‚Äî the app settled to <~1% at steady state). The optimizations objectively cut per-frame work (fewer frames + fewer particles); a real battery profile needs a physical device + a longer trace. 24fps chosen over 30 as a good smoothness/battery balance for a cozy game (tune `FrameSeconds` if you want 30 back).
#5 ‚Äî Ambient loops are SYNTHESIZED placeholders (numpy: filtered-noise rain, AM-tone crickets, swept-sine birds, an arpeggio for festival). They're functional but a bit cheesy ‚Äî real recorded/royalty-free loops would sound much better (drop them in Resources/Raw as `ambient_{day,night,rain,festival}.wav`). Couldn't verify actual audio output by screenshot; confirmed MediaPlayer runs without error.
#4 ‚Äî The emulator has no step sensor, so the Town normally shows the "no sensor / grant permission" overlay covering the base. To verify the building visuals I temporarily forced SensorAvailable/PermissionGranted true (reverted after). Also `pm grant ACTIVITY_RECOGNITION` didn't clear the permission overlay on its own. The emulator save now has a Town Hall at L3 (harmless test data; not your device).
#3 ‚Äî Live nudge firing couldn't be verified on the emulator: no step sensor means the foreground service never reconciles (so never reaches the nudge check), and the emulator clock can't be easily moved to evening. The decision logic is fully unit-tested (5 tests) and the toggle is verified; live notifications need a physical device with the service running through a day. Chose 3 sensible nudge types/thresholds (80% near-goal, 6pm streak, 5pm low-activity) ‚Äî easy to tune in NotificationPlanner constants if you want different timing.
#2 ‚Äî Did NOT create or commit a release keystore (it's a secret tied to your identity). Documented the full `keytool` + signed-AAB `dotnet publish` process in RELEASE.md instead; you generate the keystore and pass passwords via env/CI. Also wrote store-listing copy, the Play data-safety answers, and a pre-launch checklist there.
